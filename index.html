Product Story
What It Is
FORMA is a browser-native spectral morphing instrument built around a novel model: instead of oscillator banks or sample playback, you sculpt a harmonic envelope directly — placing, dragging, and removing spectral nodes that define the instrument's tonal character. A 2D morph field then interpolates continuously between spectral states in real time, creating textures no static waveform can produce.
Why It Exists
The synthesis landscape is saturated with subtractive clones and wavetable me-toos. What's missing is an approachable, professional-grade spectral instrument that doesn't require a PhD in DSP. FORMA was built for composers and sound designers who hear a texture in their mind and need a direct, tactile path to realising it — not a menu system, not a patch cable, but a surface you draw on.
Who It's For
Film composers building tension layers. Electronic producers hunting for pads that breathe differently. Sound designers who've exhausted every preset library and want instruments that respond to intention, not accident. FORMA rewards the curious: the deeper you explore the morph field, the more it reveals. It is, above all, a thinking instrument.
